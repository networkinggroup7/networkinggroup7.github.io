Module 4: Cable Testing





I. WAVES






Wave - A wave is a form of energy that travels from one point to another.

Voltage waves - is an electromagnetic force to produce electromagnetic spectrum.

Wave period - the length of time it takes for a wave to complete one loop is calculated in seconds.

Wave Frequency - this is the number of complete cycles per second, calculated in Hertz.

Pulse - If there is a disruption that lasts for a certain amount of time.







II. Sine and Square Waves






Sine Waves

The graphical representation of the general function is known as a sine wave.




Square Waves

are periodic, so they don't change over time. They keep a value for a while before abruptly changing to a new value.










III. Viewing Signals in Time and Frequency

Oscilloscope

this is an instrument that is used for displaying and analyzing waveforms of electric signals.







IV. Noise in Time and Frequency

Noise

is any disturbance that meddles with information transmission or communication and can corrupt the quality of your signal.







V. Bandwidth



Bandwidth

describes the maximum data transfer rate of a network or Internet connection.




Analog bandwidth

an electronic amplifier's analog bandwidth refers to the frequencies that are broadcast.




Digital bandwidth

this is where it measures the flow of information that can be transmitted in an amount of time.









VI. Attenuation & Insertion Loss on Copper



Attenuation

the decrease in signal amplitude over the duration of a connection is known as attenuation.




Impedance

is the calculation of the cable's resistance to alternating current (AC) in ohms.




Impedance mismatch

occurs when a connector is incorrectly mounted on a cable and has a different impedance value than the cable.




Insertion loss

is the result of the effects of signal attenuation and impedance mismatch on a communications connection.










VII. Sources of Noise on Copper Media



Noise

electrical energy on the cable called noise, which makes it impossible for a receiver to perceive data from the transmitter.




Adjacent wires

in the cable serve as antennas, receiving the transmitted energy and causing data on those wires to be distorted.




Cable tester

this is an instrument that measures the strength and connectivity of a certain type of cable.










VIII. Types of Crosstalk



NEXT (Near-end Crosstalk)

e the ratio of voltage amplitude between the test signal and the crosstalk signal when calculated from the same end.







FEXT (Far-end Crosstalk)

crosstalk that occurs farther away from the transmitter, resulting in less cable noise like NEXT.







PSNEXT (Power Sum NEXT)

calculates the total NEXT impact for all wire pairs in a cable.
